Write an article on [TOPIC] using a strict first-principles approach.

Start from the most fundamental truth of the topic and build the argument step by step. Each paragraph should logically build on the previous one, with no jumps in reasoning. Avoid surface-level explanations—focus on underlying mechanisms and why things work the way they do.

Write in simple, clear paragraphs (no bullet points, no headings). Keep the language plain, direct, and easy to follow, but intellectually sharp.

Structure the flow as follows:
1. Start by defining the topic from first principles (what it fundamentally is and why it exists).
2. Explain the current state and where most people derive value today.
3. Identify what is changing and why (technology, incentives, constraints, etc.).
4. Show how this change breaks or weakens the current model.
5. Introduce the new reality and where value is shifting.
6. Explain the implications clearly (what questions should now be asked, what mindset should change).
7. Expand to system-level thinking (how different parts connect, where gaps exist).
8. Conclude with a clear synthesis of the shift and why it matters.

Keep the writing crisp and dense. Avoid fluff, repetition, or generic statements. Every sentence should add new information or deepen understanding.

Write in a natural, conversational tone, as if explaining to a thoughtful professional. Do not use jargon unless necessary, and always keep clarity above complexity.

Match this style:
- First-principles thinking
- Each paragraph builds on the previous one
- Clear, grounded, no hype
- Focus on structural shifts and underlying mechanics
- End with a strong, clean synthesis (not motivational, but insightful)

The goal is to make the reader feel: “This made everything click.”
